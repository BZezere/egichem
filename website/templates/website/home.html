About EgiChem

EgiChem is a chemical engineering group part of associate laboratory CICECO – Aveiro Institute of Materials in the University of Aveiro (Portugal).

EgiChem is led by Professor Carlos Silva and focuses on research and development of separation processes.

Research

Supercritical Fluid Extraction Adsorption Processes Membrane Separations Ion Exchange Modeling and Simulation

Supercritical Fluid Extraction uses a fluid at supercritical conditions (usually CO2) to extract target compounds from a multitude of materials.

Our research focuses on the extraction of valuable compounds from biomass residues, characterization of the resulting extracts, and the development of industrial processes through modeling, simulation, scale-up and economic evaluation studies.

Our adsorption research has two distinct scopes: development of adsorption-based separation processes and Adsorption Heat Exchangers.

EgiChem works with Simulated Moving Bed (SMB) Chromatography for the purification of compounds with pharmaceutical interest, including the development of SMB processes and optimization strategies.

On the field of Adsorption Heat Exchangers, our work focuses on the optimization of the heating/cooling performance these equipments through modeling and simulations.

EgiChem specializes on the separation of several gases using membrane technology. Our research covers the synthesis, structural characterization, measurement of permeances and modeling of new inorganic membranes.

Our Ion Exchange research focuses on the removal of contaminants, such as heavy metals, from aqueous solutions through the development of innovative materials, including the synthesis and characterization of new ion exchange and sensing materials such as lanthanide silicates.

All research conducted at our laboratory is strongly supported by modeling and simulation. Our projects require:

  • advanced numerical methods and optimization algorithms,
  • chemical process simulation software,
  • computational fluid dynamics (CFD) simulations,
  • molecular dynamics simulations,
  • and machine learning models (for instance, artificial neural networks).
